Short version: the revision is clearly better than the original. It fixes the biggest problem, which was an overloaded Highway 1 to LA stretch, and it uses late-April Sequoia time more realistically. The main remaining issues are date alignment and a couple of drive times that look understated.

1. Is the pacing better balanced?

Yes. Much better.

Stopping in Santa Barbara on April 20 instead of forcing Cambria to LA in one day is the right correction. It turns the coast section back into a scenic trip instead of a checkpoint sprint, and giving San Francisco real time at the end is an improvement. Trimming Sequoia also makes sense for late April.

The only part that still feels a bit tight is April 22 to April 24, because LA morning + Sequoia transfer + park time, then Sequoia morning + SF transfer, compresses the inland portion.

2. Any logistical issues?

A few important ones:

1. Your weekdays are off by one day. April 17, 2026 is Friday, not Thursday. That means:
April 21 is Tuesday, not Monday.
April 22 is Wednesday, not Tuesday.
April 25 is Saturday, not Friday.

2. That changes the Griffith Observatory issue. The building is closed Mondays, but April 21 is actually Tuesday, so the original concern does not apply to that date. Also, Griffith's building opens at noon on weekdays, so "April 22 morning Griffith" only works for the grounds/views, not the full observatory visit.

3. LA to Sequoia at `~3.5h` is optimistic if you mean the actual Giant Forest / Wuksachi area. It is closer to a Three Rivers / park-entrance estimate. Once you add the mountain road into the park, it is materially longer.

4. Sequoia to SF at `~4h` is also optimistic if you are sleeping inside the park. From Three Rivers it can be around that range in light conditions, but from Giant Forest/Wuksachi you should think more like roughly `5-6 hours` door to door.

5. Santa Barbara to LA in `~1.5h` is possible in light traffic, but on a Tuesday morning into LA I would not plan the day around that best-case number.

6. Highway 1 through Big Sur is open now, but this is still a route that can see temporary storm/slide disruptions, so it needs a final Caltrans check right before travel.

3. Is 2 days enough for Sequoia?

Yes, for late April, 2 days is enough.

That's especially true because Kings Canyon's Cedar Grove road on Highway 180 is typically a May reopening, not late April, and several other seasonal roads can still be shut in spring. So you are not giving up much by dropping from 3 days to 2. One arrival afternoon, one full day, and one departure morning is enough for the core Sequoia sights.

The exception is if this is meant to be a hiking-heavy park segment. Then 2 days becomes only adequate, not generous.

4. Any issues with the Sequoia to SF drive on April 24?

Yes: the time estimate and fatigue.

If you stay inside Sequoia, April 24 is not a casual `4h` transfer. It is a real travel day. It still works, but I would treat "SF evening" as dinner / waterfront / neighborhood walk, not a major sightseeing block. Also, Sequoia roads can still have snow-related restrictions in late April, and the park notes that tire chains can be required at any time on some roads in fall, winter, and spring.

If you are staying in Three Rivers instead of deeper in the park, this day is much more comfortable.

5. Would I make any other changes?

Yes, a few:

1. Put Griffith on Tuesday afternoon/evening, April 21, not on April 22 morning. That fixes both the weekday mistake and the noon opening issue.

2. Be explicit about where you are sleeping for Sequoia. "Three Rivers" and "inside park/Giant Forest/Wuksachi" are very different for April 22 and April 24 timing.

3. Keep Highway 1 stop counts disciplined, especially on Sunday, April 19. The distances are fine, but Big Sur days run long because viewpoint stops are slow and parking can eat time.

4. If rental timing is flexible, consider picking up the car on April 18 instead of airport arrival night, and/or returning it when you get back to SF on April 24. A car in San Francisco on April 25 mostly adds parking and hassle.

5. Golden Gate Bridge sunset on arrival day is fine only if the flight lands early enough. If "arrival evening" means later evening, that stop can become rushed immediately after SFO pickup.
