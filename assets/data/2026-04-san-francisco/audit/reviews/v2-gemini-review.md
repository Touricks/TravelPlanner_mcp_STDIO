### 1. Pacing Analysis: Significantly Improved
The revised pacing is much more realistic for a "vacation" rather than a "survival exercise."

- **Highway 1:** Breaking the SF-to-LA stretch into three segments (Carmel, Cambria, Santa Barbara) turns an exhausting drive into a scenic crawl. Spending the night in Santa Barbara (Apr 20) instead of pushing into LA traffic after a full day of driving is a major improvement.
- **San Francisco:** Increasing SF time at the end (Apr 25) is a smart move. It allows for a buffer before your 10 pm flight and lets you see the city without the "arrival day" fog.
- **The LA Transition:** Spending the night of Apr 21 in LA and the morning of Apr 22 there gives you roughly 24 hours in the city. This is enough for a "Best Of" (e.g., Griffith Observatory at sunset, a quick beach walk, or a museum) without the stress of the original plan.

### 2. Logistical Issues & Drive Times

- **Highway 1 Detours (Critical):** As of 2024/2025, Highway 1 has frequent long-term closures near Big Sur (Regent's Slide/Paul's Slide). If closures persist in April 2026, you cannot drive through from Carmel to Cambria. You would have to drive Carmel → Big Sur (out and back) and then take US-101 South. Your 3-day split makes this detour much less painful than it would be in a 2-day plan.
- **LA to Sequoia (Apr 22):** The 3.5-hour estimate is optimistic. Leaving LA at 10:00 am or 11:00 am puts you in the thick of valley traffic. Budget 4.5 hours to reach the Sequoia park entrance, plus another 45–60 minutes to wind up the mountain to the Giant Forest.
- **Santa Barbara to LA (Apr 21):** 1.5 hours is only possible with zero traffic. On a Tuesday morning, plan for 2–2.5 hours via US-101.

### 3. Sequoia: Is 2 days enough?

**Yes, especially in late April.**

- **Accessibility:** In late April, the Generals Highway (connecting Sequoia to Kings Canyon) may still be under snow control or requiring chains. Higher elevation trails (like those in Mineral King or the high Sierra) will be inaccessible.
- **Scope:** You will likely spend your time in the Giant Forest (General Sherman), Moro Rock, and the Museum area. Two days (Apr 22 PM, Apr 23 Full, Apr 24 AM) is the "sweet spot" for this time of year.
- **Kings Canyon:** As you noted, Hwy 180 to Cedar Grove (the canyon floor) usually doesn't open until late May. You aren't "missing" much by cutting a day, as you'd likely be restricted to the Grant Grove area anyway.

### 4. Sequoia-to-SF Drive (Apr 24)

- **Duration:** This is a ~4.5 to 5-hour drive (250 miles). Leaving Sequoia at 1:00 pm gets you into SF by 6:00 pm, assuming typical Bay Area evening traffic.
- **Feasibility:** This is very doable. It gives you a relaxed evening in SF to return the rental car (if you choose) or park and have a nice dinner.

### 5. Other Considerations & Recommendations

- **Weather/Chains:** Even if it's 70°F in LA, Sequoia can require tire chains in April. Ensure your rental agreement allows for them, or check the NPS "Current Conditions" page daily.
- **Rental Car Logistics:** Returning the car at SFO on Apr 25 before a 10 pm flight is easy. However, parking in SF on Apr 24/25 will be expensive ($50-80/night). Consider returning the car on the evening of Apr 24 and using rideshares/public transit for your final full day in the city to save money and stress.
- **Booking:** Since you are visiting during a popular window, ensure your Sequoia lodging (Wuksachi Lodge or nearby Three Rivers) is booked well in advance, as options are limited.

**Verdict:** The revision is vastly superior. It solves the "Day 3" burnout and correctly adjusts for seasonal limitations in the Sierras.
