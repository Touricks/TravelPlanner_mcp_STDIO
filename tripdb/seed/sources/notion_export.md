### Sequoia National Park -- Vehicle & Road

| **Risk** | **Detail** | **Action Required** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tire chains in April | Generals Highway above 6,000ft may have snow/ice in late April. CA law requires chains or 4WD with snow tires when chain control is in effect. | Confirm rental car policy allows chains. Buy or rent chains in Three Rivers ($25-40). SUV with all-season tires recommended. |
| Vehicle size restriction | RVs and trailers over 22ft are prohibited on Moro Rock/Crescent Meadow Road. Standard rental cars and SUVs are fine. | No action needed for standard rental vehicles. |
| Generals Highway closure | Route 198 (Generals Highway) may have partial closures due to snow. Giant Forest area usually accessible by mid-April. | Check NPS website 1 week before: [nps.gov/seki/planyourvisit/conditions.htm](http://nps.gov/seki/planyourvisit/conditions.htm) |
| Limited food/fuel | No gas stations inside Sequoia NP. Nearest fuel in Three Rivers. Only 1 restaurant (Wuksachi Lodge) inside park. | Fill up in Three Rivers. Pack lunch, snacks, and 2L water per person for Day 6-7. |

### Highway 1 Big Sur -- Road Status

| **Risk** | **Detail** | **Action Required** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Landslide closures | Big Sur section has history of mudslide closures (most recently 2023-2026 Regent Slide). Highway 1 fully reopened Jan 14, 2026 after 3-year closure. | Check Caltrans 1 week before and morning of Apr 18: [dot.ca.gov/caltrans-near-me/district-5](http://dot.ca.gov/caltrans-near-me/district-5) |
| Narrow winding road | Big Sur section is single lane each direction with blind curves. Speed limit 25-35 mph. Fog common in morning. | Budget extra time. Do not rush. Pull over at turnouts to let faster traffic pass. |
| No cell service | Most of Big Sur has no cell coverage (AT&T, T-Mobile, Verizon). | Download offline maps before entering Big Sur. Save emergency numbers. |

### Rental Car Considerations

- **Recommended vehicle**: Mid-size SUV (e.g., Toyota RAV4, Honda CR-V). Handles Sequoia mountain roads better than sedan. Enough space for luggage on 9-day trip.
- **Tire chains policy**: **Enterprise/National/Alamo**: no chains unless required by state law. **Avis/Budget**: do not authorize chains. **Hertz**: prohibits off-paved-road use; chains not explicitly addressed. Bottom line: most companies effectively ban chains. Ask when booking and get it in writing.
- **Unpaved road restriction**: Most rental agreements prohibit driving on unpaved roads. All roads on this itinerary are paved.
- **One-way fees**: SFO pickup and return avoids one-way charges.
- **Insurance**: Consider rental company's insurance or check if your credit card provides coverage.
